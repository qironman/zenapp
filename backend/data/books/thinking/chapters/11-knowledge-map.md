# Holistic View of Human Knowledge and Wisdom

## PHASE 1: Foundational Tools (Thinking and Language)
Goal: Build a mind capable of understanding anything.
- Logic & Reasoning: Aristotle’s Organon, modern critical thinking, formal logic.
- Mathematics: Arithmetic, algebra, geometry, then calculus, probability, statistics.
- Languages: Latin and English for access to classics and modernity; etymology as cognitive glue.
- Memory and Rhetoric: Classical memory palaces, Cicero, Quintilian, modern cognitive science.

## PHASE 2: The Human Story (History, Literature, Philosophy)
Goal: Understand how we became who we are.
- Ancient Civilizations: Egypt, Greece, Rome, India, China.
- Philosophy: Plato → Kant → Nietzsche → Wittgenstein → Buddhist & Taoist thought.
- World Religions: Bible, Quran, Bhagavad Gita, Dhammapada, Tao Te Ching.
- Literature: Homer, Dante, Shakespeare, Dostoevsky, Hugo, Camus, Borges, Baldwin.
- History: From agricultural revolution to present: key epochs, revolutions, empires, ideas.

## PHASE 3: The Sciences (Nature and the Universe)
Goal: Understand how the universe works.
- Physics: Newton → Einstein → Quantum Mechanics → Cosmology.
- Chemistry: Periodic table, chemical bonding, reactions, biochemistry.
- Biology: Evolution, genetics, cellular biology, neuroscience.
- Earth & Space: Geology, climate, planetary science, astronomy.

## PHASE 4: The Modern Mind (Modern Thought & Society)
Goal: Grasp the complexities of modern life.
- Economics: Classical to behavioral to modern finance.
- Psychology: Freud, Jung, cognitive psych, trauma studies, modern neuroscience.
- Politics & Sociology: Enlightenment, nation-states, democracy, power structures, globalization.
- Technology: Industrial Revolution to AI; internet, algorithms, automation.

## PHASE 5: Applied Wisdom (Skills and Ethics)
Goal: Prepare for life, action, and moral responsibility.
- Ethics: Virtue ethics, utilitarianism, existentialism, moral psychology.
- Strategy: Sun Tzu, Clausewitz, game theory, negotiation, diplomacy.
- Crafts & Skills: Writing, public speaking, problem-solving, persuasion, creativity.
- Meditation & Resilience: Stoicism, Zen, cognitive therapy, mindfulness.

## PHASE 6: Meta-Knowledge (How to Learn Anything)
Goal: Make the student self-renewing and unconfined by the teacher.
- Learning How to Learn: Meta-cognition, spaced repetition, Feynman technique.
- Epistemology: What is knowledge? How do we know what we know?
- Limits of Knowledge: Gödel, uncertainty, paradox, paradox of progress.
- Freedom of Mind: Independent thought, skepticism, poetic imagination.
