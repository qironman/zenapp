## Essential Books on Critical Thinking (Summarized)
Title	Author	Key Ideas
Thinking, Fast and Slow	Daniel Kahneman	Two modes of thinking: fast (intuitive, error-prone) vs. slow (deliberate, rational). Key cognitive biases and heuristics.
The Demon-Haunted World	Carl Sagan	Emphasizes skepticism and the scientific method; introduces the "baloney detection kit" for evaluating claims.
Critical Thinking	William Hughes & Jonathan Lavery	Formal and informal fallacies, argument structures, and how to evaluate reasoning in real-world discourse.
Being Logical	D.Q. McInerny	A concise guide to principles of sound reasoning and common logical errors.
How to Read a Book	Mortimer Adler & Charles Van Doren	Teaches active reading and levels of reading (elementary to syntopical) to think critically about what you read.
Superforecasting	Philip Tetlock & Dan Gardner	How top predictors think: probabilistic reasoning, humility, and evidence calibration.
The Art of Thinking Clearly	Rolf Dobelli	A catalogue of cognitive biases that distort judgment, with practical advice for avoiding them.
Nonsense: A Handbook of Logical Fallacies	Robert Gula	Entertaining and rigorous examples of fallacious reasoning across contexts.
Blindspot	Mahzarin R. Banaji & Anthony G. Greenwald	Investigates unconscious biases and how they shape our thinking and decisions.
